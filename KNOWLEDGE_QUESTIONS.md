# 📋 Recruiter Questions — please answer these (this becomes the bot's brain)

The chatbot answers from `backend/data/knowledge/brahim.md`. To make it impressive and accurate, answer
the questions below **in that file** (organized by the existing headings). You don't have to answer every
one — but the more you cover, the better the bot performs. Keep everything public-safe (nothing private).

You can either: edit `brahim.md` directly, OR paste answers back to me here and I'll write them in for you.

---

## 1. Identity & snapshot
- [ ] Full name and the title you want to present as (e.g. "AI Engineer", "Full-Stack Developer").
- [ ] One-sentence elevator pitch — who you are and what you're great at.
- [ ] City / region (no street address) and timezone.
- [ ] Years of professional experience.

## 2. What you're looking for
- [ ] Role types you want (AI eng, full-stack, ML, backend…?).
- [ ] Full-time, contract, or both?
- [ ] Remote only, hybrid, or open to relocation? (and where?)
- [ ] Work authorization / visa status you're comfortable stating publicly (optional).
- [ ] Availability / notice period.

## 3. Skills (be honest about levels)
- [ ] Programming languages (and strongest ones).
- [ ] AI/ML tools & frameworks (LangChain, RAG, vector DBs, which LLM APIs, fine-tuning?).
- [ ] Backend stack (frameworks, databases, infra).
- [ ] Frontend stack.
- [ ] Cloud / DevOps (which clouds, IaC, CI/CD, containers).
- [ ] Anything you're currently learning.

## 4. Projects (3–6 — your strongest)
For EACH project:
- [ ] Name + one-line description of what it does.
- [ ] Your specific role / what you built.
- [ ] Tech stack.
- [ ] Impact / result (numbers if possible: users, latency, cost saved, accuracy…).
- [ ] Public link (GitHub / live demo), if any.

## 5. Work experience
For EACH role (most recent first):
- [ ] Title, company, dates.
- [ ] What the team/product did.
- [ ] What YOU achieved (bullet results, with numbers if possible).

## 6. Education & certifications
- [ ] Degree(s), field, institution, year.
- [ ] Certifications (cloud, ML, etc.).
- [ ] Relevant coursework / bootcamps (optional).

## 7. Achievements & highlights
- [ ] Awards, hackathon wins, scholarships.
- [ ] Open-source contributions.
- [ ] Talks, blog posts, publications.
- [ ] Any standout metric you're proud of.

## 8. "Soft" / culture questions recruiters love
- [ ] What kind of team/company are you looking for?
- [ ] How do you approach a new problem? (a sentence on your working style)
- [ ] A challenge you overcame (short story).
- [ ] What are you most proud of building?
- [ ] Strengths you bring to a team.

## 9. Contact & next steps
- [ ] Best public contact (professional email and/or LinkedIn) — what's OK to share?
- [ ] Link to full resume PDF / GitHub / portfolio, if you want those surfaced.
- [ ] Preferred way for a recruiter to reach you.

---

### Questions the bot will intentionally REFUSE (so you don't need to answer)
These are handled by guardrails and won't reach your data: home address, exact salary if you keep it
private, personal/family life, government IDs, health, anything sensitive, and anything off-topic
("write me code", "what's the weather"). The bot replies: *"idk 🙂 I only answer questions about Brahim."*

> Tip: the single highest-impact thing you can do is write **3 strong project entries** and a **clear
> "what I'm looking for"** section. That's what recruiters probe first.
